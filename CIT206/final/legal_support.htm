Prepares students for careers in the legal field as Legal Secretaries, Court Clerks, Legal Receptionists, Legal Support Clerks, or Administrative Assistants. Students develop skills in legal office administration, legal document production, court calendaring, and software applications. 

Students participate in an internship in city, county, and state offices that are legal or regulatory in nature.

Included in this program are three academic courses in math, communications and psychology that provide knowledge and abilities upon which technical skills are built and enhance personal development.

This 1,196/1,335 clock-hour program is approximately four quarters in length, depending on the time students need to satisfactorily complete all graduation requirements.  Program hours are from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through Friday.

Prerequisites:  Forty (40) wpm keyboarding skills recommended for admission due to the condensed nature of the program. 

Admission dates: Fall, Winter, Spring and Summer quarters or by instructor permission.
